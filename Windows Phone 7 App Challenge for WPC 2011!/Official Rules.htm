CONTEST DESCRIPTION:

This is a skill-based Contest open to developers in the field of software or mobile technology. The object of this Contest is to create the best application coded for Windows Phone 7 (WP7). For purposes of this Contest, each application coded on WP7 will be called an "entry". All entries will be judged using the criteria described below to determine the winners of the prizes described below.

WHAT ARE THE START AND END DATES?

This Contest starts at 12:01 a.m. Pacific Time (PT) on March 25, 2011, and ends at 11:59 p.m. PT on June 6, 2011 ("Entry Period"). Entries must be received within the Entry Period to be eligible.

HOW DO I ENTER?

To enter, you must do all of the following:

  1. Form a team of 1-2 people. Your team should have one (1) team leader. This will be the person who uploads the entry to their App Hub account and submits the entry on the team’s behalf.
  2. Upload your application to the App Hub
    1. Your entry must have passed the Windows Phone 7 application certification by June 6, 2011 to be eligible.
    2. Please note that in order to publish your apps on Windows Phone Marketplace, you need to have an App Hub account with Microsoft.
  3. Submit the Official Entry Form for the contest by visiting http://www.wp7challenge.com. You will need to provide the following information:
    1. First/Last name of the team leader/individual
    2. First/Last name of team member (if applicable)
    3. The name of your application
    4. The name of the partner / developer organization
    5. Windows Phone App Hub Product ID (optional)
    6. A summary of your application (255 characters or less)

If a team/individual is submitting multiple entries, each entry will need to be uploaded into the registration tool separately.

WINNER DETERMINATION AND PRIZES

On or around the close of the Entry Period, all eligible entries will be judged according to the criteria listed below. Judging will take place in two (2) rounds as follows:

Round 1: A panel of judges will ensure each entry meetings the minimum criteria above. Next, judges will review the application rankings and number of unique downloads, per application. The average daily downloads and average feedback rating will be used equally to determine the semi-finalists (note: if no feedback rating is available a score of 2.5 will be used). The top 50 entries will be selected as semi-finalists and will advance to round 2 of judging.

Round 2: A panel of judges will review all semi-finalist entries according to the criteria listed below and will select ten (10) grand prize winners to receive the prize listed below.

Judging Criteria

  • 30% Functionality
    • Core Functionality
    • Content Quality
    • Ease of Use
  • 40% Use of Innovative Features
    • Feature Set
    • Visual Impact
    • Stickiness
    • Originality
  • 30% Great WP7 User Experience
    • Wow Factor
    • Metro Design Language
    • WP7 Features

The decisions of the judges are final and binding. If we do not receive a sufficient number of entries meeting the entry requirements, we may, at our discretion, select fewer winners than described above.

In the event of a tie between any eligible entries, an additional judge will break the tie based on the judging criteria described above. The decisions of the judges are final and binding. If we do not receive a sufficient number of entries meeting the entry requirements, we may, at our discretion, select fewer winners than the number of Contest Prizes described below.
